Problem: We are trying to solve multiple gaming problems. These include applying realistic acceleration and decceleration, calculating turning centripetal forces and turning radius, and collision detection with complex shapes. We will apply the laws of motion and kinematic physics to solve these problems. We will probably use estimations of these laws as to conserve computing power.

Progress so far: In line with our tentative timeline, we have learned the basics of LibGDX and have imported starter code for our game. Additionally, we have also created many classes to deal with the physics that our world is subject to, from basic forces like gravity and acceleration to more advanced concepts like collision detection. We have also begun to create the track with heightmaps that will be turned into meshes that we will hopefully map textures onto to create a fully formed track. We have yet to create many models for the environment and the cars themselves, but this will come in the following week.

Preliminary Results & tentative timeline: So far, we have created a general world that our game will live in, and have yet to create the specific 3D models that will populate our game. In the next couple of weeks, the goal for this week is to create the 3D models for cars, trees, and other objects in the world. And next week, it is to integrate them into the world and try and perfect the user controls for the game.

Updated Goals: The main goal is to complete a well made game that is free of bugs and make it fun for users to play with. To do this we must accomplish multiple things:

  • Create the 3D models for cars that will populate the game
  • Integrate these models seemlessly with the game physics
  • Provide good perfomance and enforce FPS consistency, perhaps with the support of GPU
